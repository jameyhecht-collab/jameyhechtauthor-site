# Design Guidelines: Dr. Jamey Hecht Author Website

## Design Approach
**Reference-Based Approach**: Drawing inspiration from sophisticated literary and academic websites, with particular reference to established author platforms that balance scholarly gravitas with literary elegance. The design should reflect the intellectual depth of psychoanalytic and literary scholarship while maintaining accessibility for agents and publishers.

## Core Design Elements

### A. Color Palette
**Primary Colors:**
- Dark mode: Deep charcoal (220 15% 12%) with warm cream text (45 25% 92%)
- Light mode: Warm off-white (45 25% 97%) with rich charcoal text (220 15% 15%)

**Accent Colors:**
- Sophisticated burgundy (350 45% 35%) for links and highlights
- Muted gold (45 35% 55%) sparingly for special emphasis
- Subtle blue-gray (210 15% 65%) for secondary elements

### B. Typography
**Primary Font**: Crimson Text (Google Fonts) - elegant serif for body text and headers
**Secondary Font**: Source Sans Pro (Google Fonts) - clean sans-serif for navigation and metadata
**Hierarchy**: Large, generous headings (2.5rem+) with ample line spacing (1.6-1.8) for literary elegance

### C. Layout System
**Spacing**: Tailwind units of 4, 8, 12, and 16 (p-4, h-8, m-12, etc.)
**Grid**: Single-column layouts with generous margins, maximum content width of 65ch for optimal reading
**Whitespace**: Abundant breathing room between sections to create contemplative atmosphere

### D. Component Library

**Navigation**: Clean horizontal navigation with subtle hover states
**Content Cards**: Elegant publication cards with subtle shadows and typography hierarchy
**Forms**: Minimal contact forms with refined input styling
**Buttons**: Primary burgundy buttons with clean typography; outline buttons with blurred backgrounds when over images
**Bibliography Sections**: Organized by category (scholarly papers, poetry, books) with consistent metadata presentation

### E. Key Sections

**Hero**: Full-viewport section with professional author photo, name, and tagline about dual scholarly/literary identity
**Published Works**: Categorized showcase of academia.edu content with links and abstracts
**Into Theism**: Dedicated section with book description, themes, word count, and agent inquiry CTA
**About**: Academic credentials, literary achievements, and professional background
**Contact**: Literary inquiry form separate from psychotherapy practice

## Images
- **Hero Image**: Professional headshot of Dr. Hecht in scholarly setting
- **Book Mockup**: Visual representation of "Into Theism" manuscript
- **Publication Thumbnails**: Academic paper covers and book covers from existing works
- **Background Elements**: Subtle textures suggesting paper, books, or writing implements

## Visual Tone
Sophisticated academic elegance with literary sensibility - think university press website meets contemporary author platform. Clean, readable, and professional while conveying intellectual depth and creative accomplishment.
